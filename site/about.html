About this project

Building Backend is a free, open-source curriculum for learning how backends work, starting from zero. 164 lessons across 13 phases, built in the open, in Python.

Why it exists

Almost everyone starts with AI now. It is the first thing people reach for, and it is where most learning paths begin. I think that is backwards. AI sits on top of a very tall stack, and if you start at the top you never learn what is holding you up.

You cannot skip the bottom rungs of a ladder and land on the top one. You go up a step at a time. That means starting at zero, with the things nobody shows you: what is actually happening behind the scenes, what the machine is really doing, what the infrastructure underneath is for.

Get through that and AI is a fine place to end up. By then you will know what it is standing on. That is the order this was built in, and that is the whole reason it exists.

Who it is for

First, me. I built this to keep track of backend topics in one place, to go through them regularly, and to keep updating them as I learn more. It is my own record before it is anything else.

After that, anyone who wants to learn backend, or thinks they might want to. You do not need a background in it. You just need to be curious enough to start at the bottom.

This is not a mastery program and it does not promise to make you an expert. If it leaves you more curious about how backends work than when you arrived, it did its job.

Where it came from

This project began as a clone, and it would be strange not to say so. Two things shaped it.

The first is Making Software by Dan Hollick, a reference manual that explains how the software you use every day actually works. It does not teach you to build anything. It just makes you understand what is going on, and it assumes you are curious rather than technical.

The second is AI Engineering from Scratch, maintained by Rohit Ghumare and contributors, which followed that same spirit and pointed it at AI, taking a subject people find intimidating and laying it out from the beginning.

This site is a fork of that one. The layout, the structure, and the shape of a lesson are all theirs, used under the MIT license and kept with thanks. What I changed is the subject: backend engineering instead of AI. The lineage is theirs. The mistakes are mine.

Who builds it

Maintained by @ritikshub and anyone who wants to help. It is MIT-licensed and free forever. There is no token, no course upsell, and no gated content.
